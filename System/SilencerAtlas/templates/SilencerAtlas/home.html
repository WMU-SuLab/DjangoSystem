What is Silencer ?

Silencers are specialized genomic cis-regulatory DNA elements that reduce the transcriptional activity of the associated target gene, instead of increasing it. Like enhancers, silencers would provide binding sites that recruit regulatory factors, in this case as transcriptional repressors. This indicates that silencers play a vital role in regulating gene expression, especially actively participating in silencing by modifying chromatin state or occluding activators, to establish cell-type-specific expression patterns. Despite the importance of silencers in development and disease, they have been much less studied than enhancers.

What is SilencerAtlas ?

Here, we developed a comprehensive elaborately curated silencer database (SilencerAtlas,http://www.silencer/), which aims to provide a resource for experimentally supported and computationally predicted silencers, and their detailed regulatory annotation. Currently, SilencerAtlas documents a total of 13,114,740 putative silencers predicted based on high-throughput datasets (e.g.H3K27me3, H3K9me2 and H3K9me3 etc.) for 515 cell lines and 338 tissues in human. Furthermore, SilencerAtlas provides detailed genetic and epigenetic annotation for in depth analyses of silencers, including target genes, transcription factor binding sites (TFBSs), DNase I hypersensitivity sites (DHSs), CRISPR/Cas9 target sites, expression quantitative trait locus(eQTLS), SNPs. Therefore, SilencerAtlas can facilitate genomic research on silencers, and help elucidate potential biological effects in transcriptional regulation.

How can I use SilencerAtlas?

Browse sample information: The 'Data-Browse' page is an interactive and alphanumerically sortable table that allows users to instantly search for samples and customize filters using 'Data sources', 'Bio Sample type', 'Tissue type' and 'Bio Sample name'. To view silencers for a given sample, users click on 'Sample ID'.

Query silencer: Based on the tissue query, users can query the silencer for all samples of a particular type of tissue. In the gene-based query, users can query a gene of interest and SilencerAtlas will return all silencers that match the silencer-gene relationship for all samples. In the sample-based advanced query, users determine the scope of the silencer query by determining the sample and genome location for the results of interest.

Silencer details: In addition to general information about the silencer, SilencerAtlas provides detailed annotation information including TFBSs, DHSs, CRISPR/Cas9 target sites, eQTLs, common SNPs, risk SNPs, and LD SNPs.
